About Farr Yacht Design

Founded in 1972 by Bruce Farr in New Zealand, Farr Yacht Design is one of the world’s most accomplished naval architecture offices. With over 800 designs across every class and discipline, Farr has earned 43 World Championships and sustained dominance in Whitbread/Volvo Ocean Race competition. The office relocated to Annapolis, Maryland in 1986 and continues to operate under the leadership of President & Senior Naval Architect Britton Ward.

Farr’s portfolio spans superyachts, racing yachts, one-design classes, and bespoke performance vessels. Recent recognition includes the World Superyacht Award 2025 for Kiboko 4, reflecting the office’s commitment to blending radical performance innovation with refined design aesthetics. The firm is also a key technical contributor to major racing campaigns, including America’s Cup AC37 programs.

Leadership

Britton Ward

Principal, Owner & Senior Naval Architect

Britton Ward leads Farr Yacht Design as Principal and Owner, guiding the firm’s strategic direction while remaining hands-on in hull and appendage shape development, performance prediction, and focused research programs. He recently served as Hull Platform Lead for American Magic’s AC75 Patriot in the 37th America’s Cup campaign and was lead designer of the Volvo Ocean 65 class.

A graduate of Webb Institute and MIT, Britt joined Farr in 1996 and worked directly alongside founders Bruce Farr and Russell Bowler for over a decade before assuming full ownership in 2025. With over 30 years of experience, he combines deep expertise in naval architecture, hydrodynamics, and computational analysis with hands-on racing experience.

Recent Highlights

2025

Kiboko 4 Wins World Superyacht Award

Farr’s 55-meter superyacht Kiboko 4 received the World Superyacht Award 2025, recognizing its achievement in delivering radical performance innovation combined with refined design expression across all disciplines.

2024

Gelliceaux Wins St. Barths Bucket

The Farr-designed SW108 superyacht Gelliceaux dominated the 2024 St. Barths Bucket regatta in its class, demonstrating the office’s continued performance excellence in high-level competitive racing.

2024 – Present

America’s Cup AC37 Technical Contribution

Farr continues to provide technical consulting and design expertise to leading America’s Cup AC37 campaign teams, building on five decades of contribution to the world’s most prestigious yacht racing competition.

Ongoing Legacy

Volvo Ocean 65 One-Design Class

Farr designed the Volvo Ocean 65 one-design class from the ground up and continues to support fleet management. This class remains a benchmark for how professional open ocean racing can be delivered at the highest performance level.
