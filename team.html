Meet the Team

Three generations of naval architecture expertise, working from Annapolis to shape the future of sailing.

Britton Ward

Vice President & Senior Naval Architect

Britton Ward is Vice President, Senior Naval Architect and Shareholder at Farr Yacht Design. His primary responsibilities include conceptual design leadership and hull shape development of performance sailing yacht and powerboat projects, as well as management and implementation of R&D programs using state-of-the-art CFD simulation tools and HPC cluster resources.

Britt grew up in Fremantle, Western Australia, inspired by the 1987 America’s Cup. He earned his degree from the Webb Institute of Naval Architecture and a Masters in Ocean Engineering from MIT before joining Farr Yacht Design in 1996. During his time at FYD he has managed the hull research program for Oracle Racing’s 2003 and 2007 America’s Cup campaigns and was lead designer of the Volvo Ocean 65 One-Design class that debuted in the 2014–2015 Volvo Ocean Race.

Patrick Shaughnessy

President

Patrick Shaughnessy is President and Shareholder of Farr Yacht Design. Born in Annapolis, he joined the team in 1990 and has since grown through roles spanning draftsman, designer, production manager, and vice president before being elected President in 2008. He manages design sales, contract negotiations, public relations and the combined day-to-day research and design efforts of the team.

A professional-level sailor since 1996, Patrick has crewed aboard Mumm 30s, Farr 40s, TP52s, GP42s, and the 100ft Ocean Leopard. He helped set the Annapolis–Newport course record aboard CM 60 Carrera and won the 2002 Storm Trysail Club Monte-Sano Award. He travels extensively to regattas and boat shows worldwide, working closely with owners and crew to refine Farr designs.

Jim Schmicker

Vice President & Senior Naval Architect

Jim Schmicker is a Vice President and Shareholder of Farr Yacht Design. He joined FYD in 1988 specializing in rating analysis and performance optimization, drawing on degrees from the Webb Institute and MIT. His experience spans IOR 50s, Whitbread Maxis, ILC Maxis, and IMS Racers through to today’s grand-prix and cruising programs.

Jim’s responsibilities include preliminary design, concept development, hull creation, VPP updates and research. He works closely with Farr owners from initial concept through sea trials and performance optimization, often traveling to meet clients on-site and attending key regattas to ensure designs are properly prepared for competition.
