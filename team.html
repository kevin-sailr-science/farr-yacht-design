Leadership

Over five decades of naval architecture expertise, working from Annapolis to shape the future of sailing.

Britton Ward

Principal, Owner & Senior Naval Architect

Britton Ward leads Farr Yacht Design as Principal and Owner, guiding the firm’s strategic direction while remaining hands-on in all stages of design including hull and appendage shape development, performance prediction, and leading focused research programs. Under his leadership, FYD continues to push the boundaries of yacht design using the latest computational fluid dynamics tools, 3D CAD systems, and multidisciplinary optimization platforms.

Britt recently served as Hull Platform Lead for American Magic’s AC75 Patriot in the 37th America’s Cup campaign and was lead designer of the successful Volvo Ocean 65 class. He has been a core part of the design team responsible for FYD’s award-winning superyacht designs and many successful production cruising designs.

From Fremantle to Annapolis

Growing up in Fremantle, Western Australia, Britt spent his weekends racing on the Swan River, building boats, and watching the 1987 America’s Cup and Whitbread Round the World Race stopovers. These experiences sparked a lifelong passion for high-performance yacht design that led him to Webb Institute in New York, where he immersed himself in naval architecture while interning at Sparkman & Stephens and working with America’s Cup Measurer Ken McAlpine in Australia.

At Webb, Britt developed a deep appreciation for how artistry and technology combine to create well-balanced, high-performance designs. Wanting to deepen his expertise in computational and experimental hydrodynamics, he continued to MIT for a Master’s in Ocean Engineering before joining Farr Yacht Design in 1996.

Building on a Legacy

For over a decade, Britt worked directly alongside founders Bruce Farr and Russell Bowler, contributing to major racing programs including Oracle Racing’s 2003 and 2007 America’s Cup campaigns, multiple Volvo Ocean Race projects, and Vendée Globe–winning IMOCA 60 designs. He managed hull research programs and developed designs across the spectrum — from grand prix racing yachts to cruising designs and an expanding portfolio of powerboats for recreational, commercial, and military applications.

When Bruce and Russ retired in 2012, Britt stepped into the role of Senior Designer and shareholder, ensuring continuity of FYD’s design philosophy while bringing fresh approaches and cutting-edge technology to the firm’s work. In 2025, Britt took over full ownership and became Principal Designer of Farr Yacht Design.

Leading Innovation

With over 30 years of experience in marine design, Britt combines deep technical expertise in naval architecture, hydrodynamics, and computational analysis with the ability to manage complex programs within tight budgets and schedules. His unique blend of engineering rigor, design sensibility, and hands-on racing experience positions Farr Yacht Design to continue its tradition of creating beautiful, high-performance vessels that are a joy to sail.
